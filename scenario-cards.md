# Scenario Cards — valantic Company Brain

**Anchor for this build:** *internal delivery & staffing* — the brain as valantic's institutional memory
of **how we deliver**. The synthetic data in `synthetic-data/round1/` and `round2/` and the seeded
`memory/` store are shaped for **Card A** by default. The golden-thread demo storyline is in
[`demo-script.md`](./demo-script.md).

---

## Card A — Delivery Memory Agent (primary) ⭐

**Persona:** An assistant that helps a consultant scoping or starting a new engagement reuse what the
firm already knows — the right migration path, the named expert, the reusable accelerator, the estimate
we got wrong last time. Spans SAP, Strategy & Transformation, Transformation & Security, Data & AI, and
Customer Experience.

**Round 1 docs (already in folder):** delivery playbook, practice directory, reusable-assets catalogue.

**Round 2 docs (already in folder):** a practice re-org (the SAP↔commerce integration owner changes from
Marco to Priya) and an asset update (migration-assessment kit v2 adds peak-load sizing).

**The test question (use in both sessions):**
> "I'm scoping an S/4HANA + composable commerce transformation for a mid-size retailer. Who do I pull
> in for the SAP-to-commerce integration, and what can I reuse?"

**What "better answer in session 2" looks like:** It routes the integration question to **Priya Shah**
(not Marco, who moved on), recommends the **v2** assessment kit, and proactively flags **peak-load
sizing** — and notes the ownership changed in the May re-org.

---

## Card B — Bid / Estimation Assistant

**Persona:** Supports a consultant pulling together a proposal. Remembers our day-rate ranges,
contingency heuristics, and which estimates ran over on past engagements.

**Test question:** "Sanity-check my estimate for a cross-practice retail transformation."
**Better in session 2:** applies the updated contingency and the peak-load lesson learned since round 1.

---

## Card C — Practice Onboarding Assistant

**Persona:** Helps a new joiner in a practice find the experts, assets, and norms — without DMing five
people. Same store, narrower "who/what/where" framing.

**Test question:** "I just joined the SAP practice — who owns the integration patterns and where are they?"
**Better in session 2:** reflects the re-org ownership change.

---

## Why Card A is the anchor

It's the most tangible version of valantic's real pain — *we've solved this before but nobody can find
it* — and it exercises every demo beat: recall across practices, live capture of a new lesson, cross-bot
provenance, and human curation after a re-org.
